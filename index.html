 Our Story

For most of us, it all started with an unassuming mail calling for “performers, writers, actors, and theatre enthusiasts” for a stage adaptation of ‘The Metamorphosis’ by Franz Kafka. And from the first meet we had on the 1st of September, 2019, grew the Kafka@IITB Theatre Company as we set out with the intent to engage with the novella on a literal, psychological, and artistic level. Spearheaded by Palina Lissitsyna, supported by the IR-Office of IIT Bombay, and funded by the cultural department of the German General Consulate Mumbai, our group first set about establishing a vision for our adaptation. Regular reading meets were held during which we came up with varying interpretations of the novella, discussed Gregor’s relationships with the world and his family, studied other adaptations, and created the core idea of what our play was going to be. Parallelly, teams were formed to work on music, acting, writing, and other production aspects so we could bounce ideas off each other about characterisation, colour palette, voice, music, light, and sound during the ideation. Once this stage drew to a close, next came the actual work of creating the script. The writing team was soon hard at work at enjoying high teas at Gulmohar’s café and putting into words all the lofty ideas our initial meets produced. At the end of the autumn semester came the auditions for the main cast, and the new year began with a readied script and a ready cast.

Rehearsals kicked off in January 2020 and we spent the next three months working on the directing and acting aspects of the play- a long and immensely fun process that involved a great deal of repeated reminders to learn our lines, discovering ever-newer places in the campus to practice, and finding that the longer you inhabit a character, the more you begin to understand them. Around the same time, the music production kicked into high gear as the background score began to take shape. Eerie voices certainly heightened the experience of rediscovering Gregor’s metamorphosed form twice a week!

And then March marched along, and everything ground to a halt with the pandemic in full swing across the globe. Just about two weeks shy of our debut and following our first full dress rehearsal in an empty auditorium, we finally decided the safest course of action was to cancel all stage shows. We soon left Mumbai feeling appropriately dramatically despondent.

But it only took us one nostalgic Zoom meeting to collectively agree that we’d take these new developments in stride and rework our play for an audio medium. What followed were changes to the script, multiple recording sessions, mixing and mastering the audio, and many, many frozen screens, awkward pauses, and crackling voices over Zoom.

And so, after it being in the works for over 10 months in various forms, Kafka@IITB is proud to present: ‘The Metamorphosis’.

Hi, my name is Palina. I am an academic, language teacher and cultural worker.

I was teaching German at IITB for two years. I really wanted to explore Kafka with the IITB students and came up with the idea of reading “The Metamorphosis” and developing it into a play. Kafka is a timeless writer whose work is open and hermetic at the same time. We as contemporary readers can project his personal perspectives and experiences on his texts.

Working with the Kafka@IITB Theatre Company was a fantastic experience. I am enthused over the individual talents and the creativity everyone brought into the project. The most beautiful thing was that we really grew into a Kafka-Family over time

Palina Lissitsyna
Creative Director
